Experiment 01

Human-AI Co-Creation

Research focus: Exploring how to maintain artistic authenticity in the transition from analog media to generative tools.

My hand-drawn sketches — colored pencil, ink, analog textures — serve as base inputs for generative AI systems. The question isn't whether AI can enhance them, but whether the output still carries the original human intent. Each experiment below pairs the analog source with its AI-augmented result.

Base Input — Analog

Original colored pencil sketch

→
AI Output — Digital

Generative enhancement preserving original composition

Key finding

Preserving line weight and composition from the analog source maintains authorship — the AI amplifies rather than replaces the artistic decision.

Experiment 02

Interface & UX Experiments

Research focus: Investigating the role of artificial intelligence in optimizing and accelerating the product ideation process (Rapid Prototyping).

Testing how generative tools — from image models to AI coding assistants — compress the distance between concept and prototype. These experiments measure not just speed, but quality: does AI-accelerated ideation produce better design decisions, or just faster ones?

UI Concept Testing

Design system components generated and refined through AI-assisted workflows

Key finding

AI tools excel at generating variations, but the critical design judgment — hierarchy, accessibility, brand fit — remains fundamentally human. The optimal workflow is AI-generated breadth with human-curated depth.
