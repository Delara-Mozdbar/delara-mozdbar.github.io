About me

I've spent 10 years at the intersection of code and canvas — and I still get excited by a good idea.

I'm Delara, a Senior Product Designer based in Iran, open to global opportunities. My path wasn't linear — and that's exactly what makes my design perspective different.

The journey

From code to canvas

2015

Started as a Frontend Web Developer

My first years in IT — coding responsive interfaces with HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap. This technical foundation still powers every design decision I make today.

2016

Falling in love with ideas — IdeaBaran

For six months, I curated and published creative ideas at IdeaBaran — startup innovations, product concepts, and creative advertising from around the world. Long before I was a designer, I was a collector of ideas.

2018

The transition to UX Design

Joined Arsalweb as a UX Designer — creating user flows, wireframes, and prototypes for B2B, B2C, and C2C products in a fast-moving startup environment.

2020

Growing as a design leader

At Apsaaz and 9am Studio, I led UX initiatives, trained junior designers, and streamlined design processes — learning that great design is also about great collaboration.

2022

Going international — Engenesis, Australia

Designed end-to-end UX/UI flows for a Sydney-based platform, building reusable component systems and collaborating with developers across time zones.

2024

Triply, Africa + AI Labs

Currently designing for Triply in Nairobi while researching Human-AI Co-Creation — exploring how generative workflows can transform the design process itself.
